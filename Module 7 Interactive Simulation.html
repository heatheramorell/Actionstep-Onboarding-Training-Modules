The Non-Negotiable Rule
Every deal needs a logged Next Step + Close Date — always.
No deal should be in your pipeline without a scheduled follow-up within 5 business days. If a deal hasn't moved in 10 days, something is wrong. Fix it or flag it.
🌅
Protect the Morning
The first 90 minutes of each day are sacred. No slack. No email rabbit holes. Outbound activity only. This is when your best prospecting calls happen.
⚡
24-Hour Follow-Up Rule
Every meeting, demo, or discovery call must be followed by a written summary and confirmed next step within 24 hours. Slow follow-up signals low priority.
🔍
Friday Pipeline Review
Every Friday: review every open opportunity. Any deal unmoved for 10 business days needs a deliberate next action or a stage correction. No hiding in the forecast.

⚠️ Self-Created Objections — Don't Shoot Yourself

A self-created objection is pushback you generate through your own actions. Unlike natural buyer resistance, these are 100% avoidable. Know them. Eliminate them.

Self-Created #1
Skipping discovery, jumping to demo
You demo features they don't care about → "This doesn't really apply to us." Always tie every demo element to a confirmed pain.
Self-Created #2
Moving stages too early
Moving to Deal Review before gate criteria are met creates a false forecast and an unprepared close plan.
Self-Created #3
Quoting price before value is anchored
Price conversations before ROI is established always create sticker shock. Complete Gate 5 before Gate 3 matters.
Self-Created #4
Sending proposal to only one person
You will always hear "I need to get more people involved" at the last minute. Gate 9 exists to prevent this.
Self-Created #5
Not following up within 24 hours
Slow follow-up communicates low priority. "We're putting this on hold" often follows a week of silence from the rep.
Self-Created #6
Over-promising features or timelines
Promises made in sales become objections during implementation. Only commit to what's real and confirmed.
Self-Created #7
Ignoring stakeholders outside your champion
"Actually, our managing partner has concerns" is the most preventable late-stage objection. Get wide. Early.

Discovery & Deal Structure

Master the questions that reveal real pain — then structure every deal to close.

⚠ Critical Rule The #1 mistake new reps make is skipping Discovery and going straight to a demo. A demo without Discovery is a product tour, not a sale. Discovery is mandatory — no exceptions.
🔍

The DISCOVER Framework

Eight steps — from first context to committed next action

D
Diagnose the Business
Understand the firm's size, practice areas, current software, and recent history. Set context before diving into pain.
I
Identify Current State Pain
Ask open-ended questions about what is broken, slow, or frustrating today. Let them tell you — never feed them answers.
S
Surface the Impact
Quantify the pain. Time lost, revenue at risk, compliance exposure. Numbers make pain real and buildable into a business case.
C
Confirm Decision Dynamics
Map the buying group. Who is the economic buyer? Who influences? Who can block? Who owns the outcome and their career is tied to it?
O
Open Up the Future State
Ask about their vision. What does success look like? What changes for the firm if this problem gets solved in the next 12 months?
V
Validate Fit
Confirm ICP alignment, integration requirements, and timeline reality. This is where you qualify out if needed — better now than at Deal Review.
E
Earn the Next Step
Never end without a specific, committed next step. A date. An action. Both sides agree. Log it in Salesforce before you close the meeting.
R
Recap and Document
Send a written recap within 24 hours. Log full discovery notes in Salesforce. Your champion uses this to sell internally when you're not there.

"We hate our billing software." — Acknowledge and probe deeper.
IMPACT PAIN

STRATEGIC PAIN
"We can't scale without better tech." — Connect Actionstep as the growth enabler.

Stakeholder Roles at Law Firms

Know who to find, what they care about, and how to engage them

⚠ Get Wide — Always Deals die when you only have one contact. Map all stakeholders before advancing to Confirming Value. A hidden blocker discovered at Negotiation stage is a deal-killer.
💼 Economic Buyer
Managing Partner / COO
Cares about ROI, firm strategy, risk management, and final budget authority. Speak their language: outcomes and business impact, not features.
⭐ Champion
Practice Manager / IT Lead
Has a vested interest in success. Sells for you internally. Give them the language, the business case, and the tools to advocate on your behalf.
🔧 Technical Evaluator
IT Manager / Paralegal Lead
Focuses on integration, data migration, security, and support. Address their concerns directly — don't minimize technical risk questions.
💡 Influencer
Senior Associate / Billing Admin
Cares about daily usability and their specific workflows. A demo tailored to their pain builds grassroots support that champions bubble up.
🚧 Potential Blocker
CFO / Risk-Averse Partner
Driven by cost concerns, change risk, and "why now?" skepticism. Surface them early, engage their concerns directly, and avoid surprises.
👻 Unknown Stakeholder
You Don't Know Yet
Every deal has someone you haven't met. Ask your champion: "Who else will have an opinion about this?" before every stage advance.

The 5 Costliest Discovery Mistakes

Know them. Avoid them. Always.

MistakeWhy It Hurts
Demo-first sellingYou can't tailor a demo to pain you don't know. Win rates collapse against any rep who actually did Discovery.
Staying at surface pain"You have billing challenges" doesn't win budget. Impact numbers do. No quantification = no business case.
One contact onlyLaw firm decisions involve multiple partners. Hidden blockers discovered at Negotiation kill deals every time.
Vague next steps"Let's talk soon" is not a next step. Without a date and action, momentum dies and prospects go cold.
No recap emailYour champion uses your recap to sell internally when you're not there. Without it, they improvise — and get it wrong.

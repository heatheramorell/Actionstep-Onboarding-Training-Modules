Proposify: Your Proposal Tool

Learn how to create, customize, send, and track professional proposals that integrate directly with Salesforce and close deals faster.

📄

What Is Proposify?

Cloud-based proposal software integrated directly into your Salesforce workflow

Proposify is a cloud-based proposal platform that lets you build, send, and track polished, professional proposals — without starting from scratch every time. Actionstep has already built branded templates for US and UK regions. Your job is to customize and send them, primarily from within Salesforce.

💡

Pro tip: Most reps work almost entirely inside Salesforce — the Proposify widget sits right on your opportunity page. You'll rarely need to log into Proposify directly.

Why It Matters
🏆

Consistent Branding

Every proposal looks professional and on-brand — no more franken-docs cobbled together in Word.

📊

Engagement Tracking

See when a prospect opens your proposal, how long they spend on each section, and which pages they return to.

✍️

E-Signature Option

Get a formal commitment before the MSA/Order Form — ideal for complex enterprise deals where you need a staged commitment.

🔗

Salesforce Integration

Client details auto-populate from your opportunity. No re-typing names, emails, or company info.

📚

Snippet Library

Pre-written pain points and requirements you can drop in with a click — keeps language consistent across the team.

🔄

Version Control

Duplicate any proposal to create a new version when a prospect requests changes. Keep a clean history.

⚠️

Critical reminder: Proposify does NOT replace the MSA and Order Form. A signed Proposify proposal is a non-binding statement of intent only. You still need both legal documents to close. Proposify is a tool to help move deals forward — not the finish line.

Creating & Sending a Proposal

The complete workflow from Salesforce opportunity to signed proposal

⚠️

Before you start: Update your Proposify profile first — add your job title, phone number, and time zone. This info is client-facing and will appear on every proposal you send.

1

Set Up Your Profile (One-Time)

Log into Proposify → click your avatar (top right) → Profile. Fill in job title, phone number, and time zone. These populate automatically into every proposal — your client will see them on the cover page and thank-you page.

✓ Do this before sending your first real proposal. A blank phone number looks unprofessional.
2

Start from Salesforce (Recommended)

Navigate to your Salesforce opportunity → find the Proposify widget → click "Create Document." Starting here means all client details (company name, contact, email) auto-populate into the proposal — no manual entry needed.

✓ Always start from Salesforce. It saves time and avoids data mismatch errors.
⚠ If you start inside Proposify instead, you'll need to manually connect to a Salesforce opportunity. Make sure your opportunity already has a primary contact with a valid email address.
3

Select the Right Template

Choose US template (USD, US English) or UK template (GBP, UK English) based on your prospect's region. The system makes a copy of the template for you — this copy is your working proposal and won't affect the master template.

✓ Name the proposal something client-facing and clear: "Actionstep Proposal — [Firm Name]"
4

Review Auto-Populated Variables

Open the proposal editor. Yellow-highlighted text = variables pulling automatically from Salesforce and your profile. Check the cover page, introduction letter, and thank-you page. If anything looks wrong (typos, weird capitalization), fix it in Salesforce first, then click "Sync Document Data" to refresh.

✓ Click into any text box to see what's a variable (yellow) vs. editable text.
5

Customize the Firm Summary

The Firm Summary section has pain points and requirements — three bullet points each. Use the Snippets library (right sidebar) to swap in the pain points and requirements that match your prospect's situation. Highlight the text you want to replace, open Snippets, choose the right one, and it drops in.

✓ Snippets keep language consistent. Use them. Only go off-script if truly necessary.
⚠ If you need a pain point or requirement that's not in the library, flag it to Lauren or Aaron — they'll add it. Don't improvise with inconsistent language if you can avoid it.
6

Set Pricing in the Investment Table

The Investment page has a simple pricing table. Click into it to edit. You can delete rows you don't need (three-dot menu → trash). Update quantities and prices — totals calculate automatically. For complex deals, use the notes text box below the table to explain billing terms (e.g., "Billed annually," "Quarterly down payment of X").

✓ If showing monthly pricing but billing annually, note it clearly in the text box below the table — clients get confused otherwise.
7

Choose Your Next Steps / Signature Page

You have two options: With Signature (prospect signs digitally — non-binding, signals intent to proceed) or Without Signature (informational only). For enterprise deals or staged implementations, the signature page is a powerful commitment tool. For quick transactional deals, skip it. Delete the version you're not using from the left-side section list.

✓ The with-signature option is preferred in most cases — it formalizes the verbal commit and triggers a real-time notification to you when they sign.
8

Select an Implementation Page

Two options: Standard implementation overview (general timeline and process) or Partner implementation (includes a clickable link to an implementation partner's statement of work). Delete the version you're not using from the section list on the left.

✓ If a CCP (implementation partner) has provided their own SOW, link it here to keep everything in one document.
9

Do a Final Read-Through

Scroll through every page before sending. Check: Is the client name correct? Are your contact details filled in? Does pricing match what you discussed? Are there any leftover placeholder sections you forgot to delete?

⚠ Sending a proposal with "Insert Link.com" still in the button text or a blank phone number is a quick way to lose credibility. Always review.
10

Send the Proposal

Two options: Send via Proposify email (auto-addresses to your prospect, lets you add a personal note, tracks engagement) or Copy the link and paste into your own Outlook email. Either way, always toggle "Mark as Sent" — if you skip this, Proposify won't track when or how long the client views it.

✓ Send via Proposify when you want maximum tracking. Use Outlook when you prefer your own email format or have other attachments to include.
💡 Optional: Set an auto-expiry date if you have time-limited pricing. The proposal link will expire automatically and prompt the client to contact you.

Key Features Deep Dive

The tools that make Proposify worth using — and how to use them well

Engagement Tracking
📊

Once a prospect views your proposal, Proposify captures: total number of views, total time spent, time spent on each section, and number of times each section was opened. This tells you exactly what matters to them. Use it to tailor your follow-up conversation.

🟢 What You Learn

  • Spent 4 mins on pricing? They're serious but price-sensitive.
  • Skipped "Why Actionstep"? Spend more time on differentiation on your next call.
  • Read the implementation page 3x? Scope and timeline are concerns.
  • Forwarded it to someone new? Now you need to get wide — find out who that person is.

🔵 Tracking Limitations

  • Unverified viewers show as "a client" until they sign or verify email.
  • If you send via PDF download, tracking is lost entirely.
  • Must toggle "Mark as Sent" when copying link — or tracking won't activate.
  • Individual viewer ID (who specifically forwarded it) is a future feature.
Snippet Library

Snippets are pre-approved blocks of text stored in a library that you can drop into your proposal with one click. Currently organized by category (pain points, requirements). You can add snippets but should work with Lauren or Aaron to maintain consistency.

When to UseHowWhat to Avoid
Customizing pain points on the Firm Summary page Highlight text → Snippets button → pick category → click Don't improvise with ad hoc language if a snippet exists
Swapping out requirements bullets Same process as above Don't leave placeholder text that doesn't match the prospect
Need something not in the library Message Lauren or Aaron to add it Don't write one-off language that undermines brand consistency
E-Signature Page

✅ With Signature — Use When:

  • Enterprise or complex multi-phase implementation
  • Prospect needs a staged commitment before full legal docs
  • You want to trigger a real-time notification when they commit
  • Deal is at risk of going quiet after the proposal stage

⬜ Without Signature — Use When:

  • Transactional, straightforward deal
  • Prospect just wants to see pricing on paper
  • You're sending early in the process as a pricing indicator (especially UK)
  • You'll move straight to MSA + Order Form
⚠️

The Proposify signature is not legally binding and does not replace the MSA and Order Form. Always make this clear to the prospect. The signature says: "Yes, we want to move forward — prepare the formal documents."

Version Control & Duplication

If a prospect requests changes to pricing or scope after you've already sent a proposal, duplicate the original before making edits. This preserves your original version and creates a clean v2. From the pipeline view, click the proposal → Duplicate button.

Section Library

The section library (click the "+" button in the editor → Library) stores whole pages that aren't in your default template. For example: if you almost always send without a signature page but occasionally need one, you can save it to the library and drag it back in on a case-by-case basis instead of having it clutter your default template.

Real-World Scenarios

Practice the decisions you'll actually face. Click each scenario to see the situation and recommended approach.

1

The Messy Company Name

The firm name in Salesforce has weird capitalization and shows up wrong in the proposal

▶

The Situation

You open the proposal editor and notice the cover page says "JOHNSON PARKER LLC" in all caps — because that's how it was entered in Salesforce. You need to send this in an hour.

What You Do

Don't fix it directly in the proposal. Go to Salesforce and correct the company name there (change it to "Johnson Parker LLC"). Then come back to Proposify, open your proposal, and click "Sync Document Data" in the right sidebar. This pulls the corrected name and updates it everywhere in the document automatically — cover page, introduction letter, throughout. Saves you hunting through 8 pages manually.

2

The Multi-Year Pricing Question

Your prospect wants to see both monthly and annual pricing options

▶

The Situation

Your prospect says: "Can you show me what it looks like monthly vs. if we commit to 2 years?" The standard table shows one price. How do you handle this?

What You Do (Choose One)

Option A — Edit the total field: In the pricing table, click the total/subtotal cell and edit the text manually. Add a parenthetical like "(Billed Annually — $X,XXX)" to clarify billing terms.

Option B — Use the notes text box: Below the pricing table is a freeform text area. Spell out billing terms here: "Shown as monthly. Billed annually at $X,XXX. Two-year commitment pricing available at $X,XXX/year."

Option C — Ask for help: Lauren or Aaron can build a two-table layout for complex pricing if needed. Flag it before your deadline, not after.

3

The Proposal Was Opened — Now What?

Proposify shows the prospect spent 6 minutes on your proposal, mostly on the pricing page

▶

The Situation

You get a notification: the prospect viewed your proposal. You check the metrics tab and see they spent 5+ minutes on the Investment page and 30 seconds on everything else. They haven't responded to your follow-up email.

What You Do

The data tells you price is the primary concern. Don't follow up with a generic "did you get a chance to review?" email. Instead, call or email with something specific: "I noticed you've had a chance to look through the proposal — I'd love to walk through the investment section together and make sure everything aligns with your budget expectations. When's a quick 15 minutes this week?" You're using the insight to have a smarter conversation, not to reveal that you're tracking them.

4

Sign or No Signature — UK Deal

You're sending a proposal to a UK firm who asked for pricing early in the process

▶

The Situation

A UK prospect at Discovery stage has asked for "a ballpark on what this would look like." You want to send a proposal to stay professional, but they're still early in their evaluation.

What You Do

For UK prospects receiving early-stage pricing proposals: use the no-signature version. They're not ready to signal intent — they just want to see numbers. Sending a signature page at this stage may feel premature or create friction. Use the standard proposal with the "Next Steps" page (no signature) to confirm what happens when they're ready to move forward. As the deal progresses toward Deal Review or Negotiation stage, you can send an updated proposal with the signature page to formalize their commitment before the MSA and Order Form.

5

The Prospect Wants a PDF

Your prospect says "Can you just send me a PDF? We don't open links from vendors."

▶

The Situation

The prospect's IT policy apparently blocks external document links. They've asked you to send a PDF attachment instead. You know that means you lose all tracking.

What You Do

Accommodate them — use the Download PDF button in the pipeline view. Acknowledge internally that you've lost tracking and will need to rely on direct follow-up and conversation to gauge interest. If tracking is critical on this deal, try one more time: "We have a secure viewing link that doesn't require any login — many of our clients prefer it because you can share it with colleagues easily. Would that work?" If they still say no, send the PDF. Don't let a preference for tracking override the prospect's comfort.
